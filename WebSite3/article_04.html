Alistair Brownlee becomes first man to retain Olympic triathlon gold

05 Oct 2016
Category: Sport

It was a wonderful sound drifting across Copacabana beach as God Save The Queen blasted out over the sand. And, with Sugarloaf Mountain providing a sumptuous background, there was the Union flag hanging from not one but two poles. What a sight it was. What a moment of uplift. And what a performance it marked. Alistair Brownlee had just won gold in the Olympic triathlon, while his brother Jonny had come in second.

After London, when Alistair had won and Jonny finished third, this was the moment that lifted the charming, self-effacing, endlessly mutually supportive siblings into sporting immortality. “We’ve done it,” said Jonny. “One and two in the Olympics, we couldn’t do better than that.”

And the thing was, never for a moment was it in doubt. Not with the Brownlees. Not with the very epitome of Yorkshire grit. The pair led the race from start to finish before collapsing over the line in an emotional bundle of achievement, Alistair clutching a Union flag in one hand and a Yorkshire standard in the other.
